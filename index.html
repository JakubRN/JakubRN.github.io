New York City 2015 Green Taxi Trip Data Visualisation

A brief outline over the fundamentals of our idea and visualization are given below in order to provide an introduction. A thorough analysis of the project can be found in our Explainer Page.

Explainer Page Github repository Online website

What do you see?

The visualization starts with a projection map of NYC where two sets of clickable circles are added to pinpoint centers of areas with high pickup and dropoff activity. Color coding is applied on the map so that each hue is consistent to the number of people per square kilometer in the respective borough. As you can notice, the density of transportation activity becomes massive close to Manhattan, with four pickup centers surrounding it and one located in it. Therefore don’t be surprised to learn that Manhattan has twice the population per square kilometer than Queens and Brooklyn where the population in absolute number is higher than Manhattan.

Two sets of buttons included to enable double filtering the results by narrowing down the datapoints of interest to only one quarter of the year, or between two different time intervals, one for day and another for night hours. Finally there are two timelines where you can see the trip count timeline filtered by your selection; if no selection is active then the timelines reflect all 2015 data. It is worth noting that the upper timeline shows the total count, while the lower can be filtered to display only day-hour or night-hour trips.

How it works?

Initially, before any selection is carried out by means of clicking some button the visualization reflects results over both day-time and night-time trips throughout 2015. You are able to activate either one button or two buttons (one in each set) simultaneously. The cluster centers and timelines will be updated according to your selection.

Furthermore when clicking on a cluster center on the map, e.g. a pickup center, red lines will connect it to all dropoff centers; in this case the width of each line reflects what portion of pickups from the clicked center led to dropoffs in the other cluster.
Note that if you have two buttons activated simultaneously, then you’ll see the actual datapoints projected on the map in addition to the lines.

What is its purpose?

Observe the density and flow of transportations in NYC, locate the centers of massive transportation activity and quantify the proportion by which these centers communicate with one another. View the variations in pickup and dropoff densities between different seasons or during day or night hours. See the weekly oscillations on trip count and how they are distorted at certain periods.
